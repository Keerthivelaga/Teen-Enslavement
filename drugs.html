Substance Awareness

Drug Types & Their Effects

Understanding the dangers of different substances is the first step toward prevention and recovery.

💉
Injecting Drugs
Injecting drugs causes severe long-term vein damage, risk of gangrene, and bloodborne infections including HIV, Hepatitis B & C. Even the smallest amount of infected blood can transmit life-threatening viruses. Never share equipment, always use sterile needles.
Extreme Risk
❄️
Methamphetamine (Ice)
A central nervous system stimulant known as 'speed', 'goey', or 'ice'. Synthetically made and highly potent. Can be smoked, injected, swallowed, or snorted. All forms carry severe health risks including psychosis, heart attack, and stroke.
Extreme Risk
🌿
Cannabis
Comes from Cannabis sativa containing THC. Available as marijuana, hashish, or oil. Known as 'grass', 'pot', 'weed', and 'ganja'. Regular use affects memory, coordination, and mental health, especially in developing teenage brains.
High Risk
💊
Ecstasy (MDMA)
A man-made drug with both stimulant and hallucinogenic properties. Often called 'E', 'pills', or 'eccy'. Composition varies unpredictably. Causes dangerous overheating, heart problems, and severe dehydration that can be fatal.
Extreme Risk
🍄
Hallucinogens
Also called 'psychedelics', they distort perception, thinking, time, and emotions. Includes LSD and magic mushrooms. Can trigger lasting psychological trauma, persistent flashbacks, and severe anxiety disorders long after use.
High Risk
🤍
Cocaine
A stimulant made from coca plant leaves. Known as 'coke', 'Charlie', or 'snow'. Often mixed with other substances. Causes severe cardiovascular problems, addiction after first use, and devastating financial and personal consequences.
Extreme Risk
😮‍💨
Inhalants
Volatile substances like petrol, glues, and spray paints inhaled for a high. Causes confusion, nausea, and drowsiness. Sudden Sniffing Death Syndrome can occur on the very first use. Causes irreversible brain damage.
Extreme Risk
💉
Sedatives (Benzodiazepines)
CNS depressants like Valium and Mogadon, prescribed for stress and anxiety. Misuse leads to dependency. Combining with alcohol dramatically increases overdose risk. Withdrawal without medical supervision can be life-threatening.
High Risk
💿
Heroin & Opioids
Heroin is an illegal opioid made from morphine. Opioids depress the central nervous system and are highly addictive. Overdose is the leading cause of drug-related death. Purity varies wildly, making every dose potentially fatal.
Extreme Risk
🆘
