What is Addiction?
Addiction is a chronic, relapsing brain disease characterized by compulsive drug seeking and use despite harmful consequences. It rewires the brain's reward system, making recovery a genuine medical challenge — not just a matter of willpower.
Signs & Symptoms
Watch for: preoccupation with the substance or behaviour, restlessness when unable to engage, physical dependence, denial of the problem, increasing tolerance requiring larger doses, and withdrawal from family and friends.
Treatment Approaches
Effective treatment combines medical detox, medication-assisted treatment (MAT), professional counseling, therapeutic support groups, and behavioral therapies — all tailored to the individual's circumstances and needs.
Physical Addiction
Involves the brain's chemical response to substances, causing cravings and withdrawal symptoms when use stops. Common examples: alcohol, opioids, cocaine, and tobacco. Physical symptoms emerge without the substance.
Psychological Addiction
Results from emotional factors — feelings of euphoria, escape from stress, or compulsive behavior cycles. Examples include methamphetamine, caffeine dependency, and anxiety medications used beyond prescription guidelines.
Comorbid Addiction
Addiction co-occurring alongside mental health conditions. Examples include depression with substance use disorder, bipolar disorder with compulsive gambling, and anxiety disorders paired with eating disorders.
Behavioural Addiction
Addiction to activities rather than substances — gambling, gaming, social media, or eating disorders. The brain responds to these behaviours the same way it does to drugs, creating compulsive cycles that are hard to break.
Support Groups
Find strength in community. Support groups connect you with others sharing similar struggles — a place to share experiences, receive encouragement, and build accountability that significantly improves recovery outcomes.
Self-Help Books
Numerous books offer evidence-based insights and practical guidance for overcoming addiction. They provide valuable perspectives and strategies to supplement professional treatment and enhance your recovery journey.
Medication-Assisted Treatment
MAT combines evidence-based medications (like Suboxone and Vivitrol) with behavioral therapy to address both physical and mental aspects of addiction. Proven to dramatically increase the likelihood of sustained, long-term recovery.
Professional Counseling
Working with a licensed mental health professional helps address the psychological and emotional root causes of addiction. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and other evidence-based approaches have strong success rates.
